Annotated bibliography tips
----------------------------

https://owl.purdue.edu/owl/general_writing/common_writing_assignments/annotated_bibliographies/index.html

1. Summarize
    * What type of source is this? (Article, book, video, etc.)
    * Who wrote it and why?
    * What information or arguments are presented in this source?
    * What is the scope of this source?
2. Assess
    * What is this source useful for, and how useful is it?
    * What biases does the author have? Are they explicit or implicit?
    * Is it a trustworthy source?
    * What are the limits of this source?
3. Reflect
    * How does this fit into your research goals?
    * What can this source teach you for future use?
    * Does this source offer a new way of thinking about your topic?
    * How does this source help you shape your argument?
